Picks / What the vienna.rb Team Thinks Is Worth Sharing This Week

01/01 yesterdays picks!

In a series on this website we’ll entertain YOU with our picks - or: what we think is worth sharing - every week. Books, articles, gems, fun stuff… you’re in for an eclectic mix! So, here’s for the thirty-sixth edition:

Laura:
  • Let’s Talk About Clients - Oldie but Goldie: Michael Bierut’s talk at the NYC Creative Mornings
  • Vmail - A Vim Interface to Gmail
  • Girls haven’t been hacking for the last 10 years - A good article focusing on the heart of the problem
Alex:
  • SVG Drawing Animation - Line drawing animation with illustration fade-in
  • Haml’s little-known list_of helper - The list_of method will iterate and yield the results of the block into sequential list elements.
  • Polybox - A combo of Docker and Vagrant to make full dev environments easily available. Prestocked with boxes for Rails 4, Django, Go, Haskell, and Nodejs.
Ben:
  • Removing the GIL in Ruby - Eliminating Global Interpreter Locks in Ruby through Hardware Transactional Memory
  • Ruby 2.1 Garbe Collectors - nice write up of the changes in Ruby 2.1
  • The Lost Art of C Structure Packing
