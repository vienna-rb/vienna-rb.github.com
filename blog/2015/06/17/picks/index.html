Picks / What the vienna.rb Team Thinks Is Worth Sharing This Week

06/17 Picks!

In a series on this website we’ll entertain YOU with our picks - or: what we think is worth sharing - every week. Books, articles, gems, fun stuff… you’re in for an eclectic mix! So, here’s for the one-hundred-eleventh edition:

Laura:
  • MetroGit - Paris Metro stations visualised as a git graph
  • First Pull Request - A nifty tool to see the first pull request you ever sent on GitHub.
  • Cutting Corners or Why Rails May Kill Ruby - A very interesting and to-the-point article about “monkey-patching all the things” and why it might be terrible for our ecosystem, by Piotr Solnica
Floor:
  • Fonts that actually work together - Skillcrush helps the non-design-savvy with free font pairings that WORK.
  • apipie - Apipie is an interesting tool/gem for documenting your Rails API.
  • rice steamer - don’t think on it, simply register for this new event format at sektor5. Zero regrets guaranteed.
Gerald:
  • FizzBuzz @ Rosetta Code - the “classic” exercise in hundred of (inspiring) versions
  • FizzBuzz In Too Much Detail - write-up about the Fizz Buzz exercise in Ruby – from simple “classic” style to fully “modern” functional-style with clear separation of side effects (i/o functions)
  • Ruby Books Page @ Planet Ruby - summer beach reading recommendations
  • The Architecture of Open Source Applications - inspiring (free) book series about studying real world (patterns) code (applications) incl. Volume I, Volume II, Performance Edition and the upcoming 500-Lines-or-Less Edition
