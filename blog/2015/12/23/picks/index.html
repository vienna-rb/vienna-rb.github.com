Picks / What the vienna.rb Team Thinks Is Worth Sharing This Week

12/23 Picks!

In a series on this website we’ll entertain YOU with our picks - or: what we think is worth sharing - every week. Books, articles, gems, fun stuff… you’re in for an eclectic mix! So, here’s for the one-hundred-thirty-eighth edition:

Laura:
  • Junior developer - What does it mean? A look at the responsibilities, rather than the skillset, of a junior developer
  • Shrine - There’s a new file uploader in town! Meet Shrine.
  • 32C3 - Chaos Communication Congress is starting this weekend. Some of the Viennese Rubyists will be there – come say hello! Or if you can’t attend, here is a link to the recordings and live stream.
Markus:
  • The Inescapable Pragmatism of Procedures - Avdi Grimm on the strength and weaknesses of a functional vs. a procedural approach to problems.
  • The Jacob’s Ladder of coding - “Anecdotes and questions about climbing up and down the ladder of abstraction” (plus some interesting tidbits about old computer hardware).
  • Ignorance Beats Empathy - Mike Monteiro on how to achieve better empathy towards users in design.
Ramón:
  • Codecademy’s AngularJS course - I’m about halfway through and have found this helpful and a lot of fun!
  • Christmas Tree Formatter for Rspec - Run your tests festively!
  • RubyMotion motion-support gem - Like ActiveSupport, but for RubyMotion!
