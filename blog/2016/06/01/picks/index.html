Picks / What the vienna.rb Team Thinks Is Worth Sharing This Week

06/01 Picks!

In a series on this website we’ll entertain YOU with our picks - or: what we think is worth sharing - every week. Books, articles, gems, fun stuff… you’re in for an eclectic mix! So, here’s for the one-hundred-sixty-first edition:

Laura:
  • Rails Has Won: The Elephant in the Room - Highlighting why Rails was a success and still looking at the issue critically, Fabio Akita wrote an article that couples nicely with Piotr Solnica’s post shared last week
  • Pushing RubyGems.org forward - For all of us who use Ruby Gems on a (probably) daily basis, Nick Quaranto wrote a post about the current state of RubyGems.org and helping it go forward by contributing to the project, giving feedback on policies, and more.
Markus:
  • Learn Lua in 15 Minutes - Lua is a lovely dynamic language that, due to its size, is a favorite for embedding, while still offering a lot of performance. And now you can also learn it in 15 minutes!
  • Updating a classic - Charles Stross updated the OSA’s “Simple Sabotage Field Manual” from WW2 to modern times, so you can sabotage a rival tech start-up. “It’s often hard to know where incompetence ends and malice begins”
  • How to get anything done? - 4 tips - Robert Pankowecki with 4 very practical tips to get shit done.
Max:
  • Abstractions and the role of a framework - Nice follow up to last week’s extensively debated My time with Rails is up post, sharing thoughts on the different levels of abstraction and how frameworks come into play there.
  • Your Program is a Special and Unique Snowflake - Deciding on the level of abstraction on an even lower, per line level.
  • How to Win the Coding Interview - Advice to survive and actually understand whiteboard and co.
Pilar:
  • Gitfiti - Pixel art in your git history - because why not.
  • You Feel Like Shit: An Interactive Self Care Guide - An interactive guide to help you read internal signals and take care of yourself.
