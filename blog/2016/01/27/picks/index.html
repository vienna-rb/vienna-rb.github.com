Picks / What the vienna.rb Team Thinks Is Worth Sharing This Week

01/27 Picks!

In a series on this website we’ll entertain YOU with our picks - or: what we think is worth sharing - every week. Books, articles, gems, fun stuff… you’re in for an eclectic mix! So, here’s for the one-hundred-forty-third edition:

Laura:
  • TrumpScript - It’s not Ruby but it’s still too great not to share.
  • Lotus is now Hanami - Lotus has recently been renamed; read all about it in this blog post.
Ramón:
  • Nativefier - Run web apps as if they were native! Runs on Electron.
  • RubyMotion: MotionKit - Really helpful DSL for setting up view layouts. No need for Interface Builder files!
  • Influencing Web Layouts with Print Layouts - I found this an interesting read on using more magazine-styled layouts.
Markus:
  • On Ergonomics - Why an ergonomic working place is important (unless you like constant pain)
  • Conceptual Debt is Worse than Technical Debt - Yes, there’s something even worse than technical debt out there.
  • How to trick a neural network into thinking a panda is a vulture - Neural networks can do amazing things, but they can be tricked into seeing things that aren’t quite what they seem.
Max:
  • The Bike Shed 48: “Is Everyone Trying Their Best?” - Software is broken - Derek and Sean on why it’s not fun to be one of the developers who just don’t care.
  • Switching from rbenv to postmodern’s ruby-install and chruby - Remind the unix philosophy and ditch rbenv (or rvm) for something more lightweight.
